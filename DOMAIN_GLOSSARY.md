# Domain Glossary

## Bounded Context Vocabulary

### 1. Semantic Metrology
The practice of utilizing Geometric Dimensioning & Tolerancing (GD&T) principles to define LLM prompts as rigid, mathematical blueprints rather than fluid conversational text. Ensures zero-entropy structural output.

### 2. Ontological Shear
The systemic error or geometric misalignment that occurs when an AI agent's interpretation of a fluid human semantic concept breaks against the rigid binary requirements of the local execution codebase or business logic.

### 3. S5-Modal Attention
An architectural pattern mapping standard attention matrices to S5 Kripke frames using topological regularizers. Prevents Semantic Annihilation by retaining contradiction in superposition, essential for holding multiple contradictory stakeholder requirements in tension.

### 4. Golden Scar Protocol
An epistemic mechanism that assigns a mathematical weight of Φ (1.618, the Golden Ratio) to the dominant human empirical frame and 1.000 to the stochastic AI generation frame. Used to preserve formal human logic and resolve Ontological Shear within agentic topologies.

### 5. Paraconsistent Annotated Logic
A paraconsistent logical framework used to tolerate, evaluate, and contain contradictions within multi-agent consensus without allowing the system to experience a classical "Logical Explosion" (ex falso quodlibet).

### 6. Xenolinguistic Risk
The risk that an LLM's continuous latent space will hallucinate discrete structural keys or invent new, unrecognized domain terminology, thus shattering deterministic JSON/YAML schemas and failing programmatic validation.

### 7. Resolution Collapse
A failure state where floating-point inaccuracies at the exact zero-boundary of a geometric manifold cause the system to hallucinate false positive interferences or incorrectly declare a conflict resolved.

### 8. Prompt Dimensioning & Tolerancing (PD&T)
A framework that defines personas and instructions via Datums, Form Controls, and Tolerances, turning natural language into measurable, immutable Feature Control Frames.
